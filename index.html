AI Agents as Learning Tools, Not Replacements

I use AI as a thinking partner to challenge assumptions, visualize edge cases, and accelerate learning. Instead of replacing human judgment, these tools help me ask better questions, explore alternative solutions, and understand complex systems faster. The goal isn't automation‚Äîit's augmentation.

My approach: Treat AI as a collaborative thought partner. Use it to stress-test ideas, generate multiple perspectives, and learn faster‚Äîbut always apply critical thinking to validate outputs. The human remains the decision-maker.

From Research to Revenue: Lessons in Data Translation

Moving from academic research at Technion to business analytics taught me the critical skill of data translation. In research, accuracy is everything. In business, actionable insights matter most. The challenge is maintaining rigor while delivering speed. My background in psychology helps bridge this gap‚Äîunderstanding both the data and the humans who need to act on it.

Building Products with a Psychology Lens

Studying psychology taught me that people rarely behave rationally‚Äîthey follow patterns, habits, and emotional triggers. Great products respect this. Rather than forcing users to adapt to complex interfaces, we should design systems that align with natural human behavior. Technical excellence matters, but empathy-driven design wins every time.
